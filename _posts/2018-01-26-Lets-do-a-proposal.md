# Project idea
<br>
 A **identified company** security is preparing to enter the cybersecurity market and have already made dramatic claims surrounding its product which they describe as a hardware-based approach to enterprise cybersecurity. **Identified company** claims they can offer National Intelligence grade solutions to enterprise customers for securing data in flight. The company additionally claims that this supposed hardware barrier leverages quantum-resistant encryption and True Random Number generated symmetric keys.
 The company has been awarded a **identified USPTO Patent**. This project will verify the claims made by this company by reverse engineering the USPTO documents and supplement data and create a working practical copy or this cryptographic technique.
<br>
## Motivation for our idea and what we expect to learn from it
<br>
This project will introduce the team members to industrial cutting edge techniques being explored for enterprise networks on a large commercial scale. Following the approval of a patent **identified company** will be launching in the next few months following validation of there technical claims. While a working hardware model can not be obtained for this project the concept within the technical patent application will allow an understanding of this technique and allow now uses to be identified. Additional motivation for this project was to discover how the claim made by **identified company** can be taken seriously without having a full understanding of their methodology, by reverse-engineering the provided patent can the technique be used for other applications outside of physical hardware barriers as defined within there patient application.
<br>
